## What I worked on

My work at Tampere University was tied to GPT Lab Seinäjoki and practical applied AI. The useful questions were rarely just "can the model do this?" They were closer to "can this workflow help a company understand, test, or improve something real?"

That led to prototypes around language models, computer vision, optimization, reporting, and software engineering workflows. Some work was technical exploration, some was communication and events, and some was the quieter job of making the results understandable enough that someone else could evaluate them.

## Why it mattered

The research assistant role gave me a good bridge between experiments and practical software. It made AI feel less like a separate category and more like another engineering material: powerful in the right place, expensive or fragile in the wrong place, and only useful when it fits a real workflow.
