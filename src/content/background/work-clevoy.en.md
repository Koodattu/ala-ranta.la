## What I focus on

Clevoy is the current ownership-heavy part of my work. The role combines product direction, technical architecture, implementation, infrastructure, and the everyday tradeoffs that come with turning a product idea into something people can use.

The useful part is staying close to the whole path. Decisions about mobile clients, backend services, admin tooling, deployment, and cost all affect each other, so the work is less about isolated features and more about keeping the system coherent as it grows.

## What this has taught me

The main lesson is that technical leadership works best when it is still connected to the build. Architecture, planning, and delivery stay healthier when the person setting direction also understands where the code is sharp, where operations are fragile, and where users actually feel the product.
